# Hydro Jetting vs Snake Drain Cleaning

Hydro jetting and mechanical drain snaking are two common methods used to remove blockages from plumbing systems.

Both techniques restore wastewater flow, but they use different tools and are suited for different types of blockages.

---

## What Is Drain Snaking?

Drain snaking uses a mechanical auger to break up or remove blockages inside pipes.

A flexible cable with a rotating head is inserted into the drain. As the cable moves through the pipe, it breaks apart clogs and pulls debris out of the line.

Drain snaking is commonly used for:

- Hair clogs
- Food buildup
- Small drain blockages
- Minor pipe obstructions

This method is often effective for localized clogs.

---

## What Is Hydro Jetting?

Hydro jetting uses high-pressure water to clean the inside of plumbing pipes.

A specialized hose delivers water at very high pressure, which removes:

- Grease buildup
- Sludge
- Mineral deposits
- Tree roots
- Other debris inside pipes

Hydro jetting cleans the entire pipe interior rather than just breaking through a clog.

---

## Key Differences Between Hydro Jetting and Drain Snaking

| Feature | Drain Snaking | Hydro Jetting |
|-------|---------------|---------------|
| Method | Mechanical auger | High-pressure water |
| Primary purpose | Break up clogs | Clean pipe walls |
| Best for | Localized blockages | Heavy buildup or grease |
| Cleaning effectiveness | Partial pipe clearing | Full pipe cleaning |

---

## When Each Method Is Used

Drain snaking is often used when:

- The clog is small or localized
- Pipes are older and may not tolerate high pressure
- A quick clog removal is needed

Hydro jetting is often used when:

- Pipes contain heavy grease buildup
- Sewer lines are heavily clogged
- Pipes need full cleaning rather than simple blockage removal

A plumbing professional may inspect the drain before determining the best method.

---

## Summary

Both hydro jetting and drain snaking are effective methods for clearing plumbing blockages.

Drain snaking breaks apart clogs using a mechanical cable, while hydro jetting uses high-pressure water to clean pipe interiors. The appropriate method depends on the type and severity of the blockage.
