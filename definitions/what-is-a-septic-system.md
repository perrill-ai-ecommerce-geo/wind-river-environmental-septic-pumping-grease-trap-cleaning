# What Is a Septic System?

A septic system is an on-site wastewater treatment system used by homes and buildings that are not connected to a municipal sewer system. Septic systems treat and dispose of household wastewater directly on the property.

Septic systems are commonly used in rural and suburban areas where centralized sewer infrastructure is not available.

---

## Main Components of a Septic System

A typical septic system includes three primary components:

**1. Septic Tank**

The septic tank is a buried container that receives wastewater from the home. Inside the tank:

- Solids settle to the bottom as sludge  
- Grease and oils float to the top as scum  
- Liquid wastewater flows out to the drain field

**2. Drain Field (Leach Field)**

The drain field is a network of perforated pipes buried in soil. It distributes wastewater from the septic tank so it can be naturally filtered by the surrounding soil.

**3. Plumbing Connections**

Pipes carry wastewater from sinks, toilets, showers, and appliances into the septic tank.

---

## How a Septic System Works

A septic system works through a combination of settling, biological treatment, and soil filtration.

The process typically follows these steps:

1. Wastewater flows from the home into the septic tank  
2. Solids settle while grease floats to the surface  
3. Liquid wastewater exits the tank into the drain field  
4. Soil filters and treats the wastewater before it returns to groundwater

---

## Why Septic Systems Require Maintenance

Septic systems require periodic maintenance to operate properly.

Maintenance typically includes:

- Septic tank pumping
- Septic system inspections
- Monitoring drain field performance

Without regular maintenance, solids can accumulate and cause system failure.

---

## Where Septic Systems Are Common

Septic systems are commonly used by:

- Rural homes
- Suburban properties outside sewer coverage
- Small commercial buildings
- Agricultural properties

These systems provide an effective wastewater treatment solution when municipal sewer infrastructure is unavailable.
