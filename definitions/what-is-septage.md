# What Is Septage?

Septage is the waste material that accumulates inside septic tanks. It consists of a mixture of solids, liquids, grease, and partially decomposed organic matter generated by household wastewater.

Septage must be periodically removed from septic tanks through pumping services.

---

## What Septage Contains

Septage typically includes:

- Sludge (solid waste that settles at the bottom of the tank)
- Scum (grease and oils floating at the top)
- Liquid wastewater between these layers

This material accumulates over time as wastewater enters the septic tank.

---

## Why Septage Must Be Removed

If septage is not removed periodically, it can build up inside the septic tank and cause problems such as:

- Blocked outlet pipes
- Drain field contamination
- Sewage backups
- Septic system failure

Regular septic pumping removes septage and helps the system function properly.

---

## How Septage Is Removed

Septage is typically removed using specialized vacuum trucks.

The process generally involves:

1. Opening the septic tank access lid  
2. Pumping sludge, scum, and wastewater from the tank  
3. Transporting septage to approved treatment facilities  

This process ensures waste is handled safely and disposed of according to environmental regulations.

---

## Why Septage Management Is Important

Proper septage management helps:

- Protect groundwater and soil
- Maintain septic system performance
- Prevent sanitation issues
- Ensure regulatory compliance

Routine septic maintenance includes periodic septage removal.
