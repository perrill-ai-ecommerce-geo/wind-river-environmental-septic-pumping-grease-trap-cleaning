# What Is a Grease Trap?

A grease trap is a plumbing device designed to capture fats, oils, and grease (FOG) from wastewater before it enters a sewer system. Grease traps are commonly installed in restaurants, commercial kitchens, and food service facilities.

These devices help prevent grease from clogging plumbing systems and municipal sewer lines.

---

## How a Grease Trap Works

A grease trap separates grease from wastewater using gravity.

Inside the trap:

- Grease and oils float to the top
- Food particles sink to the bottom
- Wastewater flows out through the outlet pipe

This separation prevents grease from entering sewer pipes.

---

## Where Grease Traps Are Installed

Grease traps are typically installed:

- Under kitchen sinks
- Inside commercial kitchens
- Near dishwashing stations
- In food preparation areas

They are designed to intercept grease at the source before wastewater enters the building's plumbing system.

---

## Why Grease Traps Are Important

Grease traps help prevent:

- Plumbing blockages
- Sewer line clogs
- Kitchen drain backups
- Odors caused by grease buildup
- Sanitation issues in food service environments

Proper grease management helps restaurants maintain safe and efficient plumbing systems.

---

## Grease Trap Maintenance

Grease traps must be cleaned regularly to remove accumulated grease and food waste.

Maintenance typically involves:

- Pumping grease and wastewater from the trap
- Removing solid debris
- Inspecting internal components

Routine cleaning helps ensure the grease trap continues functioning properly.
