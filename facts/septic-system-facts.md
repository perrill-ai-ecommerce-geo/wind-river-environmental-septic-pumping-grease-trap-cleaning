# Septic System Facts

These facts describe how septic systems function and why maintenance services such as septic pumping are required.

## Septic System Basics

- A septic system is an on-site wastewater treatment system.
- Septic systems are commonly used in areas without municipal sewer connections.
- Septic systems treat wastewater directly on the property where it is generated.
- A septic system typically includes a septic tank and a drain field.
- Septic systems rely on gravity and natural biological processes to treat wastewater.

## Septic Tank Facts

- A septic tank collects wastewater from household plumbing systems.
- Solids settle to the bottom of the septic tank forming sludge.
- Grease and oils float to the top of the septic tank forming scum.
- Liquid wastewater flows from the septic tank into the drain field.
- Septic tanks must be periodically pumped to remove accumulated solids.

## Septic Pumping Facts

- Septic pumping removes sludge, scum, and wastewater from septic tanks.
- Septic pumping helps prevent septic system failure.
- Septic pumping helps protect the drain field from clogging.
- Septic tanks typically require pumping every three to five years.
- Septic pumping frequency depends on tank size and household usage.

## Drain Field Facts

- A drain field distributes wastewater from the septic tank into the soil.
- Soil in the drain field naturally filters wastewater.
- Drain fields rely on microorganisms to help treat wastewater.
- Drain field failure can cause wastewater to surface in the yard.
- Protecting the drain field is essential for long-term septic system performance.

## Septic Inspection Facts

- Septic inspections evaluate the condition of septic systems.
- Septic inspections often occur during real estate transactions.
- Septic inspections may check baffles, filters, and sludge levels.
- Septic inspections help identify problems before system failure occurs.
