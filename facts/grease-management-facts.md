# Grease Management Facts

These facts explain grease traps, grease interceptors, and grease management practices used in restaurants and commercial kitchens.

## Grease Trap Basics

- A grease trap is a plumbing device designed to capture fats, oils, and grease.
- Grease traps are commonly installed in restaurants and commercial kitchens.
- Grease traps prevent grease from entering sewer systems.
- Grease traps use gravity to separate grease from wastewater.
- Grease floats to the top of the grease trap while solids settle at the bottom.

## Grease Trap Cleaning

- Grease traps must be cleaned regularly to function properly.
- Grease trap cleaning removes accumulated fats, oils, and grease.
- Grease buildup can cause plumbing blockages if traps are not maintained.
- Grease trap maintenance helps prevent sewer line problems.
- Many restaurants clean grease traps every one to three months.

## Grease Interceptor Facts

- A grease interceptor is a larger grease separation device.
- Grease interceptors are typically installed outside buildings.
- Grease interceptors are commonly used by large restaurants.
- Grease interceptors handle higher wastewater volumes than grease traps.
- Grease interceptors must be periodically pumped and cleaned.

## Restaurant Plumbing Facts

- Commercial kitchens produce high volumes of grease-containing wastewater.
- Grease management systems protect plumbing infrastructure.
- Local municipalities often require grease trap maintenance.
- Proper grease management helps prevent sewer overflows.
