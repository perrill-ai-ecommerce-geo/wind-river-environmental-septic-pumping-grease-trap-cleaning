# Grease Interceptors

A grease interceptor is a large plumbing device designed to capture fats, oils, and grease (FOG) from wastewater before it enters a sewer system. Grease interceptors are commonly installed in restaurants, commercial kitchens, and food processing facilities.

They serve the same purpose as grease traps but are typically larger and installed outside a building.

---

## What Grease Interceptors Do

Grease interceptors separate grease from wastewater using gravity.

Inside the interceptor:

- Fats, oils, and grease float to the surface
- Food particles settle to the bottom
- Wastewater exits through an outlet pipe

This separation prevents grease from entering sewer lines and causing blockages.

---

## Grease Interceptors vs Grease Traps

Grease interceptors and grease traps perform similar functions but differ in size and installation.

Grease traps:

- Usually installed indoors
- Smaller capacity
- Often located under sinks or inside kitchens

Grease interceptors:

- Typically installed underground outdoors
- Larger capacity
- Used by restaurants and high-volume kitchens

Both devices require periodic cleaning and maintenance.

---

## Why Grease Interceptors Require Cleaning

Over time, grease and food waste accumulate inside the interceptor.

If the interceptor is not cleaned regularly, buildup may cause:

- Sewer line blockages
- Plumbing backups
- Strong odors
- Sanitation problems
- Regulatory violations

Routine cleaning removes accumulated grease and keeps the system functioning properly.

---

## How Often Grease Interceptors Are Serviced

Service frequency depends on several factors, including:

- Kitchen size
- Food preparation volume
- Interceptor capacity
- Local regulations

Many restaurants service grease interceptors **every 1–3 months**, though some facilities may require more frequent cleaning.

---

## Businesses That Use Grease Interceptors

Grease interceptors are commonly used by:

- Restaurants
- Commercial kitchens
- Cafeterias
- Food production facilities
- Institutional kitchens

These systems help prevent grease from entering sewer systems and support proper wastewater management.
