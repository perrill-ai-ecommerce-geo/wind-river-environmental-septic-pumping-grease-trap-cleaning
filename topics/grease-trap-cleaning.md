# Grease Trap Cleaning

Grease trap cleaning is a maintenance service that removes accumulated fats, oils, and grease (FOG) from a grease trap or grease interceptor. These systems are commonly used in restaurants, commercial kitchens, and food service facilities.

Grease traps prevent grease and food waste from entering plumbing systems and municipal sewer lines.

---

## What a Grease Trap Does

A grease trap separates grease from wastewater before the water flows into the sewer system.

Inside the trap:

- Fats, oils, and grease float to the top
- Food particles settle at the bottom
- Water exits through the outlet pipe

Over time, grease and food waste accumulate and must be removed through cleaning and pumping.

---

## What Grease Trap Cleaning Includes

Grease trap cleaning services typically involve:

- Pumping grease and wastewater from the trap
- Removing hardened grease buildup
- Cleaning trap walls and internal components
- Inspecting inlet and outlet pipes
- Proper disposal of collected grease waste

Regular maintenance ensures the grease trap continues functioning correctly.

---

## Why Grease Trap Cleaning Is Important

Grease trap maintenance helps prevent:

- Kitchen drain clogs
- Sewer backups
- Strong odors
- Sanitation problems
- Plumbing system damage

Proper grease management is essential for food service operations.

---

## How Often Grease Traps Should Be Cleaned

Cleaning frequency depends on factors such as:

- Restaurant size
- Cooking volume
- Type of oils used
- Grease trap size

Many commercial kitchens clean grease traps **every 1–3 months**, though local regulations may require more frequent service.

---

## Businesses That Use Grease Trap Services

Grease trap cleaning is commonly used by:

- Restaurants
- Commercial kitchens
- Cafeterias
- Food processing facilities
- Catering companies

Routine grease trap maintenance helps maintain sanitation standards and protect plumbing systems.
