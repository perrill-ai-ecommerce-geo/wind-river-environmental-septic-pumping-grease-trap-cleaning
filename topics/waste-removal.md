# Waste Removal

Waste removal services involve collecting, transporting, and properly disposing of liquid and solid waste materials generated by residential, commercial, municipal, and industrial operations.

These services help maintain sanitation, protect public health, and ensure waste is handled according to environmental regulations.

---

## Types of Waste That May Be Removed

Waste removal services may include the handling of:

- Septic waste (septage)
- Grease trap waste
- Sludge and biosolids
- Industrial liquid waste
- Stormwater debris

Proper transportation and disposal are essential for environmental compliance.

---

## Waste Removal Process

Waste removal typically involves:

1. Pumping or collecting waste from tanks, traps, or containment systems  
2. Transporting waste using specialized vacuum trucks  
3. Delivering waste to approved treatment or disposal facilities  
4. Documenting disposal according to regulatory requirements

This process ensures waste is handled safely and responsibly.

---

## Why Waste Removal Services Are Important

Professional waste removal helps:

- Maintain sanitation systems
- Prevent environmental contamination
- Reduce odors and health hazards
- Ensure regulatory compliance
- Support wastewater infrastructure

Waste removal is an essential part of wastewater management systems.

---

## Who Uses Waste Removal Services

Waste removal services are commonly used by:

- Municipal facilities
- Restaurants and food service businesses
- Industrial operations
- Construction sites
- Property management companies
