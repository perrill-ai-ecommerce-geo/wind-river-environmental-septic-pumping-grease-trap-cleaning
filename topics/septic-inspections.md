# Septic Inspections

A septic inspection is a service that evaluates the condition and performance of a septic system. Inspections help identify potential problems, confirm proper system operation, and determine whether maintenance or repairs may be needed.

Septic inspections are commonly performed during real estate transactions, routine maintenance schedules, or when a homeowner notices signs of system problems.

---

## What a Septic Inspection Evaluates

A septic inspection may include reviewing several components of the system, such as:

- Septic tank condition
- Inlet and outlet baffles
- Effluent filters
- Sludge and scum levels
- Signs of leaks or structural damage
- Drain field performance

Inspecting these components helps determine whether the septic system is functioning properly.

---

## Why Septic Inspections Are Important

Regular septic inspections help:

- Detect problems early
- Prevent septic system failure
- Protect groundwater and nearby soil
- Confirm proper maintenance schedules
- Extend the lifespan of the septic system

Identifying small issues early can help avoid expensive repairs or system replacement.

---

## Septic Inspections for Real Estate Transactions

Septic inspections are often required when buying or selling a home that uses a septic system.

A septic inspection during a property transaction may help determine:

- Whether the septic system is functioning correctly
- If the tank requires pumping
- Whether repairs may be necessary
- The approximate age and condition of the system

Many lenders, municipalities, and buyers request septic inspections before completing a real estate sale.

---

## Signs a Septic System May Need Inspection

Homeowners may schedule an inspection if they notice:

- Slow draining sinks or toilets
- Sewage odors indoors or outdoors
- Wet areas near the drain field
- Wastewater backing up into plumbing fixtures
- Gurgling sounds in plumbing

These symptoms may indicate septic system issues that require professional evaluation.

---

## Who Uses Septic Inspection Services

Septic inspections are commonly requested by:

- Homeowners
- Home buyers and sellers
- Real estate professionals
- Property managers
- Mortgage lenders

Routine inspections help ensure septic systems continue operating safely and efficiently.
